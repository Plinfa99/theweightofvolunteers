How the percentages were calculated

Most values in this visualization are not directly measured, but estimated using a mix of official data and survey results. Here’s how the numbers were derived:

  • Volunteer numbers: Based on the Freiwilligensurvey 2019, 28.8 million people volunteer in Germany. We used reported shares per field (e.g. 14.6% in education) to estimate volunteer numbers by sector. [8]
  • Employee numbers: Taken from Bundesagentur für Arbeit (April 2025) reports. Sectors like health, education, and public safety where matched. [3]
  • Volunteer hours: Estimated from survey data:
    • 60% volunteer ~2h/week
    • 23% volunteer ~4h/week
    • 17% volunteer ~7h/week
    → Weighted average: ~3.9 hours/week per volunteer [8]
  • Employee hours: Assumed average weekly hours:
    • 39h in most sectors [7]
    • 41h in justice/police [6]
    • 46h in education [4]
    • 35h in environment/infrastructure [5]
  • Example calculation:
    If 4.2 million volunteers work in education and contribute ~3.9h/week, that's 16.38 million hours.
    If 1.74 million teachers work ~46h/week, that’s 80.04 million hours.
    → Volunteers = 14.8% of total hours in education.
  • Exception: Civil protection values (96.6% volunteers, 72.2% of hours) are based on official data from the ZiviZ/BBK Sonderauswertung (2023). [1]
